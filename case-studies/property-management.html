The Client

The Snapshot

A residential property management company in Tampa, Florida, managing 187 units across 14 properties. A mix of single-family rentals and small multifamily buildings.

4 employees: the owner, one leasing agent, one maintenance coordinator, and a part-time office admin.

The owner, Jennifer, started 11 years ago as a real estate agent. Took on a few rentals for clients. Then a few more. Now she runs a business she never planned to build and can't step away from.

Software: Buildium for accounting and tenant portal, Google Sheets for "everything else," WhatsApp for maintenance emergencies, and a growing sense that none of it actually talks to each other.

The Situation

What Was Actually Happening

The Maintenance Chaos

"Requests come in from everywhere. Buildium portal, email, text messages, WhatsApp, sometimes a tenant just calls my personal cell. My maintenance coordinator has three different lists going and things still fall through. Last month a tenant posted a one-star review because a leak took 9 days. We thought it was fixed on day 2."

Maintenance is the #1 source of stress across the industry. Jennifer was spending 20+ hours a month just tracking requests across channels, not even fixing them.

The Tenant Screening Trap

"I approved a tenant last year. Perfect application, verified income, great references. Three months later, eviction process. Turns out the paystubs were fake. I still don't know how I missed it."

70% of property managers report an increase in fraudulent applications. Only 25% have any systematic detection method. Jennifer was doing it manually, trusting documents that looked right until they weren't.

The Owner Report Burden

"I have 6 property owners who expect monthly reports. Every month I spend basically an entire day pulling numbers from Buildium, formatting them in Google Docs, and answering follow-up questions that could've been in the report. One owner calls me every week asking things I've already sent him."

The reports were taking time away from operations, and owners still weren't satisfied. 40% of property managers say they lack visibility into strategic decisions, and their property owners feel it too.

The Owner Trap

"I got into this for the flexibility. Eleven years later, I check my phone before I get out of bed. I handle 'emergencies' on vacation. My husband jokes that the tenants are my real family. He's not entirely joking."

She became a business owner by accident, and now works more hours than any of her employees. Jennifer was closer to 55 hours a week.

“

The maintenance requests used to run my life. Now they run through the system. I still see everything, but I only touch what actually needs me. That's the difference between managing properties and being managed by them.

— Jennifer, Owner, PM Firm
What We Installed

The Systems

01

Single Intake Queue

Every maintenance request — portal, email, text, WhatsApp, voicemail — enters one queue. No more three lists. No more "I thought you had it."

02

Auto-Triage + SLA Alerts

Requests auto-sorted by urgency. Leaks and HVAC get flagged immediately. Routine requests get 48-hour SLA. Owner gets alerted only when something breaches SLA, not for every ticket.

03

Tenant Communication Bot

Trained on the 30 most common questions: "When is rent due?" "How do I submit a maintenance request?" "What's the pet policy?" Handles 60%+ of inbound messages. Escalates to human when needed.

04

Vendor Coordination Tracker

Every work order has clear owner, scheduled time, and status. Jennifer can see where every request stands without calling her maintenance coordinator. Vendors get automated reminders.

05

Tenant Screening Workflow

Document verification flags, income consistency checks, reference follow-up automation. Doesn't replace human judgment — but catches the obvious fakes before they get to Jennifer's desk.

06

Owner Report Automation

Monthly reports generated automatically from Buildium data. Sent on the 5th of every month. Owners can see occupancy, maintenance summary, rent collection, and upcoming renewals — without calling Jennifer.

07

Lapsed Tenant Re-engagement

Identifies tenants who haven't renewed or have gone quiet. Automated check-ins before lease end. Flags high-value tenants for personal outreach. Turned "we lost them" into "we kept them."

08

Review Recovery System

Follow-up after maintenance completion. Happy tenants get prompted for Google review. Unhappy tenants get routed to Jennifer before they post publicly. Caught 6 potential negative reviews in the first 60 days.

09 Core System

Owner's Daily Brief

Morning summary: what needs attention today, what's overdue, what's coming this week. Email triage suggestions. No more checking 4 apps before coffee.

The Stories Behind the Numbers

The Leak That Didn't Become a Review

Week 2, a tenant reported a bathroom leak via WhatsApp at 9pm. Old system: it would've sat in Jennifer's personal messages until morning. New system: auto-logged, flagged urgent, vendor notified by 9:15pm. Fixed by noon next day. Tenant left a 5-star review mentioning "fast response." That review would've been 1 star.

The Fake Paystub Catch

First month, screening workflow flagged an application with income docs that didn't match employer verification. Jennifer would've approved it manually. The PDF looked perfect. Estimated cost avoided: $4,000+ in eviction and vacancy costs.

The Owner Who Stopped Calling

One property owner called Jennifer 3–4 times per month asking about rent collection and maintenance status. After automated reports started, calls dropped to once per month and it was actually about strategy, not status updates.

The Renewals That Almost Didn't Happen

Lapsed tenant system identified 23 tenants whose leases were ending within 90 days and who hadn't responded to renewal notices. Personal outreach converted 19 of them. At ~$4,000 turnover cost per unit, that's over $75,000 in avoided vacancy and re-leasing costs.

The Sunday Morning Test

Jennifer tracked her time for a month. Before: checking messages by 7am, including weekends. Maintenance "emergencies" interrupting family time. After: Sunday check-in takes 10 minutes. Real emergencies get routed automatically. Everything else waits for Monday.

“

Last Sunday I took my family to brunch. Didn't check my phone once. My husband asked if I was feeling okay. That's how different things are now. The emergencies still get handled. They just don't need me anymore.

— Jennifer, Owner, PM Firm
