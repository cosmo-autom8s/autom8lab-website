The Client

The Snapshot

A specialty diagnostics laboratory in South Florida with three collection sites across the region. 60+ employees ranging from lab technicians and phlebotomists to front desk staff, a small sales team, and one very stretched marketing person.

They run two businesses under one roof:

Direct-to-Consumer Testing

Self-pay customers ordering panels online. Biohacker Performance. Longevity & Aging. Women's Hormone Optimization. Gut Health. The kind of testing people can't get from their PCP.

Practitioner Partnerships

Functional medicine doctors, naturopaths, nutritional coaches, and high-end personal trainers who send their clients in for bloodwork. 50+ active partners at the time we started.

They were growing. But the founder couldn't tell you why they were growing, or which parts of the business were actually working.

The Situation

What Was Actually Happening

The Partner Blind Spot

"We had 50+ practitioners sending us patients. If you asked me which five were actually driving revenue, I couldn't tell you. I found out a partner switched to a competitor when I ran into them at a conference. Three months after they stopped referring."

No tracking. No automated thank-you notes. No system to notice when someone went quiet. Partners were earning referral fees, but most patients were not properly reporting and we didn't know why some partners stopped sending people.

The Attribution Gap

"We were spending $11,400 a month on Google Ads. Patients were showing up. But when I asked 'did this person come from Google or from Dr. Martinez's referral?' nobody could tell me. We knew the clicks. We had no idea about the actual patients."

Sometimes a patient would mention they were referred and ask for the referral discount. That's how we knew. Otherwise it was a guess. Marketing couldn't prove ROI. Partners couldn't see their own numbers. Everyone was flying blind.

The Call Center Bottleneck

"Our call center was answering the same four questions 30 times a day. 'Do I need to fast?' 'What's in the hormone panel?' 'Do you take insurance?' 'Where do I park?' Meanwhile, we had a list of patients who hadn't come back in six months that nobody had time to call."

Every inbound call meant someone wasn't doing outbound. The team knew there was revenue sitting in lapsed patients, but they were too busy answering basic questions to go after it.

The Founder Trap

"I started this lab because I'm good at diagnostics. Somewhere along the way I became a guy who spends his evenings reviewing marketing reports he doesn't understand and answering emails about vendor contracts."

Tuesday nights were "marketing review" sessions. Weekends were catching up on the emails that piled up during the week. The business was running him.

“

I used to find out we had a problem when revenue dropped. Now I find out when a partner misses their usual monthly volume by 20%. That's a Tuesday morning Slack alert, not a quarterly crisis.

— Founder, Specialty Diagnostics Lab
What We Installed

The Systems

01

Partner Intelligence Dashboard

See exactly who's sending patients and who stopped. Automated alerts when a partner's volume drops. Partners can log in and see their own referral numbers and earnings.

02

AI Support Handler

Trained on their 47 most common questions. Handles insurance, prep instructions, panel details, parking, hours. Escalates to human only when needed. Freed up the call center to do outbound.

03

Patient Follow-Up System

Automated check-ins after appointments. Asks how the experience was. Happy patients get prompted to leave a Google review. Unhappy patients get routed to the team to fix the issue before it becomes a public complaint.

04

Lapsed Patient Outreach

Identifies patients who haven't returned in 90+ days. Sends personalized reminders. Flags high-value patients for the call center to reach out directly. Turned dormant patients into recurring revenue.

05

Full Attribution Tracking

Every patient tagged with their source. Google ad? Partner referral? Walk-in? Finally know which marketing dollars are working and which partners are actually driving business.

06

Staff AI Training

Trained the team on how to use AI tools in their daily work. Faster email responses. Better patient communication. More efficient admin work. The whole operation got sharper.

07 Core System

Founder's Daily Brief

Morning briefing with what actually needs attention. Email triage. Meeting prep. Research on demand. The founder stopped being the bottleneck for every decision.

08

Competitor Intelligence Alerts

Weekly briefing on what competitors are doing. New panels they're launching, pricing changes, marketing moves. The founder stopped finding out about competitor changes from partners and started knowing first.

09

Cash Flow Forecasting

Weekly projections based on booked appointments, partner pipeline, and seasonal patterns. The founder stopped wondering if next month would be tight and started planning three months ahead.

The Stories Behind the Numbers

The Recovered Partners

First month, we identified 11 partners who'd gone quiet in the last 90 days. The team reached out. 7 started sending patients again. One said he thought they'd closed their Miami location. Another said he just forgot about them because nobody had reached out in months.

The Partner Revenue Unlock

Once partners could see their own numbers, referrals went up. Partners started promoting the lab more actively because they could finally see what they were earning. One functional medicine doctor doubled her referrals within 60 days after getting access to her dashboard.

The Review Turnaround

The follow-up system caught 4 unhappy patients in the first month before they posted negative reviews. The team fixed the issues directly. Meanwhile, happy patients started leaving Google reviews without being asked in person. Review volume went from 3–4 per month to 12–15.

The Call Center Flip

With 73% of inbound questions handled by AI, the call center finally had time to work the lapsed patient list. First month, they reactivated 23 patients who hadn't been in for 6+ months. That's revenue that was just sitting there, waiting for someone to make the call.

The Tuesday Night Test

The founder tracked his time for a month. Before: 12+ hours per week on admin, marketing review, email catch-up. After: 5.5 hours. Tuesday night "marketing sessions" are gone. He's back to thinking about growth instead of firefighting.

“

My favorite part? I stopped being the person who answers 'what's included in the Longevity Panel?' eight times a week. That's handled. Now my team has time to actually call the patients who haven't come back in six months. That's where the money is.

— Founder, Specialty Diagnostics Lab
